Media Wall Design Guide for Islamabad Homes

Marble-panel media walls, fluted columns and lighting explained — how to size and style a TV wall for your Islamabad or Rawalpindi living room.

The media wall — a designed, built-out wall built around the television — has become one of the most requested living room upgrades in Islamabad. Instead of a TV mounted on a plain painted wall, a media wall turns the whole area into a styled focal point, combining panel finishes, columns, and lighting into one cohesive design.

What Is a Media Wall?

A media wall is a custom-built feature wall designed around your television, usually combining a central marble-effect or UV sheet panel with side elements like fluted wood-tone columns, mirror strips, or inlay detailing. Unlike a simple wall panel, a media wall is planned as a complete composition — proportioned around your TV size and the wall's dimensions, not just a repeating pattern.

It hides wiring, gives you a place to route your set-top box and speaker cables neatly, and instantly becomes the room's focal point when guests walk in.

Popular Media Wall Styles

Our most-requested style is the classic symmetrical media wall — a white marble centre panel flanked by dark wood-tone fluted columns and mirror strips, which works in almost any living room. For a more modern look, the modern media wall pairs a white marble panel with vertical gold and black inlay strips against a wood-tone fluted side panel.

If you prefer understatement, a minimalist media wall — a single marble panel between slim fluted columns with one linear wall light — gives a clean, contemporary result without feeling busy. For a bolder statement, the luxury media wall uses a black marble panel with gold veining framed by fluted columns and wall sconces, ideal for larger, more formal living rooms.

Sizing a Media Wall to Your Room

The right media wall size depends on your TV size and the wall's total width and height, not a fixed template. As a general guide, the central panel should extend comfortably beyond the TV on both sides so the screen doesn't look cramped, and side columns or elements should be proportioned to the ceiling height — a low ceiling needs a more restrained design than a double-height living room.

We combine wall panels (usually WPC) and UV sheet finishes to build each media wall, so we can match the exact marble tone, gold veining, or wood finish you want — see our wall panels guide for more on panel types.

What Installation Involves

A media wall is a bigger job than a single flat panel, since it usually includes a built-out frame, mixed materials, and wiring management for the TV, set-top box, and any wall lighting. Our team measures your wall and TV on-site, plans the panel layout and column spacing, then builds and finishes the wall in place. Most media walls take a few days from measurement to completed install, depending on complexity and lighting.
